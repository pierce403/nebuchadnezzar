Validator

Overview

Objective

  • As hashrate is traded from sellers to buyers in the Hashpower Marketplace, there is a critical function called "Validation" that ensures that the promised Hashrate purchased is delivered.

  • This Validation function has traditionally been performed in two ways:

    • As the Buyer Node (running full Lumerin Wallet Desktop), or

    • Centralized Lumerin.io Validator service (if you purchase contracts, as a buyer, on the Lumerin Hashpower Marketplace)

  • In order to open up the Marketplace and truly become decentralized, we need to allow this Validator function to be performed by other teams on the network (not just the central Validator).

  • The latest update (1/15/20225) enables this functionality where an interested party can stake their Lumerin, Setup a Proxy-Router and "become a Validator" on the Hashpower Marketplace!

Incentives and Punishment

  • Running a Validator node is a critical component of the Hashpower Marketplace and in doing so can be rewarding (incentives) but bad behavior will be punished to ensure good faith health and activity in the network.

Incentives

  • With the staking of at least LMR 1,000,000 to your validator contract,

    • When a hashrate contract is purchased and

    • Your node is selected as the Validator

    • You will earn 1% LMR of the price of the contract.

  • eg: Currently, a 7 day, 500TH/s contract is offered at 15,000 LMR....as a validator, you would earn 150 LMR if the contract ran it's full duration and hashrate.

  • So, as long as the hashrate marketplace is active (miners and sellers are offering contracts) AND your Validator node is available, then you will earn a return on your Lumerin Validator Stake!

  • There is no "base incentive" for Validator staking ... the two conditions above must be true to start getting rewards...but the more you validate, the more you earn!

Punishment

  • In order to keep the network free from bad actors, besides the initial Validator Stake, the network has put in place mechanisms to punish bad behavior or nodes that are not well maintained or available.

  • When there are at least 4 Validators on the network, each of the Validator Peers will start polling the others every 5 minutes.

  • If a peer is found to be offline or unreachable on that poll cycle, a validatorComplain entry is submitted to the smart contract

  • If three (3) validatorComplaints are received by separate peer validators, then the PunishAmount(Currently at 100,000LMR) will be deducted from the offending Validator's stake and sent to the Validator contract (community)

  • If this Punishment happens ten (10) times, then the Validator's stake will drop below the `PunishThreshold' of 100,000LMR and the validator will be DeRegistered and not available.

  • A peer validator cannot submit multiple validatorComplaints in a row against the same offline validator so the network disables the ability to force another validator out of the network.

  • In order to begin Validating again, the punished validator will have to re-stake the minimum amount.
